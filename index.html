Himani Garg

Assistant Professor in Heat Transfer
Department of Energy Sciences, Lund University

I study how turbulence moves heat — and how we can make it do so better. My group uses high-fidelity simulation, in particular Large Eddy Simulation (LES) and Direct Numerical Simulation, to understand turbulent heat transfer over rough and additively manufactured surfaces, internal cooling of gas turbine blades, viscoelastic and elastic turbulence in microchannels, and two-phase flows in hydrogen technologies such as PEM fuel cells and electrolyzers.

I hold a PhD in Fluid Mechanics from Université de Lille (2019) and have held research positions at Université Grenoble Alpes, the Okinawa Institute of Science and Technology, and Lund University. My work is supported by grants from the Hains Foundation, the Crafoord Foundation, and Kempestiftelserna, and I am a co-applicant of the Vinnova AdTherM competence centre on advanced computing for sustainable thermal management in industry.
