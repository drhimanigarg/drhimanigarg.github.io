The Swedish Mechanics Days (SMD 2026) took place at Lund University on 10–12 June, and our group was there in force with six contributions.

I presented our work on turbulent heat transfer over rough surfaces, and my students and collaborators presented studies on steel plate cooling (P. De Rijcke), bubble size distributions in alkaline electrolyzer anode channels (C. Rissler), conjugate heat transfer and premixed flame dynamics in a step combustor (M. Ly), matrix channels for internal turbine blade cooling (R. Seppey), and CFD-plus-experimental optimization of matrix cooling (C. Dogget and co-workers).

It is always special to see the group's breadth on display at a national meeting — and even more so on home ground.
