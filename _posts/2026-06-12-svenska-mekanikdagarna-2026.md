The 29th Swedish Mechanics Days (SMD 2026) took place at Lund University on 10–12 June, and it was a special edition for us — a national mechanics meeting on home ground, and our group contributed with six presentations spanning the full breadth of our research.

![Presenting the local impact of roughness topography on heat transfer in turbulent flow over rough surfaces](/assets/img/smd2026-talk.jpg)

I opened our contributions with a talk on the local impact of roughness topography on heat transfer in turbulent flow over rough surfaces, and also had the pleasure of chairing Session 5C. The group's presentations:

- *Numerical investigation of turbulent heat transfer over rough surfaces* — H. Garg
- *Numerical simulation of the cooling for steel plates* — P. De Rijcke and H. Garg
- *Numerical investigation of matrix channel for internal turbine blade cooling* — R. Seppey, H. Garg, and M. Mihaescu
- *CFD and experimental optimization of matrix cooling* — C. Doggett, J. Müller-Wusterwitz, F. Maletzke, N. Rignell, J. Soriano Lluch, and H. Garg
- *Large eddy simulation of conjugate heat transfer and premixed flame dynamics in a rearward-facing step combustor* — M. Ly, T. Nilsson, H. Garg, D. Lörstad, and C. Fureby
- *Bubble size distributions in alkaline electrolyzer anode channels: effects of oxygen injection patterning* — C. Rissler, H. Garg, and M. Andersson

It was a proud moment to see our PhD students Pierre, Romain, Mattias, and Christian, along with our M.Sc. project team, present their work at a national meeting — several of them for the first time.

Beyond our own sessions, the conference offered three days of stimulating presentations and discussion, including the traditional Odqvist lecture by Staffan Lundström (Luleå University of Technology) and plenary lectures by Elena Gutierrez Farewik, Stefan Lindström, and Clara M. Velte on topics ranging from porous media flow and movement biomechanics to fatigue and unstable turbulence.

Many thanks to the local organising committee at LTH for a very well-run conference — details and the book of abstracts are available on the [SMD 2026 website](https://www.smd2026.se/). We look forward to the next Swedish Mechanics Days at Linköping University!
