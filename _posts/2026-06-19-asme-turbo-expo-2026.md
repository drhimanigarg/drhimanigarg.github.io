The AdTherM research group and I had the pleasure of participating in ASME Turbo Expo 2026 in Milan between 15 and 19 June.

![The AdTherM team at the entrance of ASME Turbo Expo 2026](/assets/img/turboexpo2026-group.jpg)

We were proud to attend with our team of nine — PhD students and seniors — and to contribute to the conference with six presentations, showcasing the results of our ongoing research activities. Kudos to Mattias Ly and Romain Seppey for the insightful presentations they gave!

Our papers at the conference:

- **GT2026-175585** — *Numerical Investigation of Turbulent Heat Transfer Over Rough Surfaces at Medium–High Prandtl Numbers*, by Himani Garg.
- **GT2026-175582** — *Large Eddy Simulation Study of Conjugate Heat Transfer and Premixed Flame Dynamics in a Rearward-Facing Step Combustor*, by Mattias Ly, Thommie Nilsson, Himani Garg, Daniel Lörstad, and Christer Fureby.
- **GT2026-177503** — *Turbulent Heat Transfer in Lattice-Structured Matrix Cooling Channels*, by [Himani Garg](https://www.linkedin.com/in/himani-garg-4977b0256/), Romain Seppey, Mihai Mihaescu, and Christer Fureby.
- **GT2026-176392** — *Numerical Investigation of Heat Transfer Rates and Pressure Drop in a Matrix Channel for Turbine Blade Internal Cooling*, by [Romain Seppey](https://www.linkedin.com/in/romainseppey/), Himani Garg, and Mihai Mihaescu.

Beyond the technical sessions, Turbo Expo once again proved to be an invaluable opportunity to reconnect with friends and colleagues from around the world, exchange ideas, and strengthen our research network through both longstanding collaborations and new connections.

![Colleagues and collaborators at ASME Turbo Expo 2026](/assets/img/turboexpo2026-team.jpg)

A big thank you to the organizers, presenters, and all the researchers who made this event such a stimulating and inspiring experience. We return home energized, with new ideas, fresh perspectives, and an even stronger motivation for the work ahead.
